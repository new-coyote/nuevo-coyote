In the above video, I explain a phenomenon I've been seeing over and over for years. With the current climate of misogyny, racism, fascism, and an overall disrespect for workers and humanity in general, it's rearing its ugly head everywhere. 

I call it The Pat Principal, after the well known management concept called [The Peter Principal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_principle). In the Peter Principal, workers get promoted to the level of their incompetence. This means that a talented worker will get promotions until they are so far away from their skillset that they become an incompetent, stagnant manager. 

In the Pat Principal, we see a skilled and talented manager, usually a woman, a person of color, or someone from another marginalized group come into an organization will all the management skills they need. However, their obvious talent threatens or makes their managers uncomfortable, either because the managers have internalized sexism, racism, etc,. because the managers are incompetent, or some combination of both. 

These men in management, who are not used to being uncomfortable for any amount of time, will coach their new employee to be less direct, less precise, to speak less to their fellow workers about how to be more efficient. They will say or imply that the new employee is "too aggressive," they will tone police her and stop her from meeting her KPIs so that she can be a better "culture fit." For the organization, which has a culture of racism, sexism, and general lack of achievement.  

If the new employee does what her male supervisor asks her to do, she becomes worse at her job, but better at making him feel good about himself. Because her male supervisor is putting his personal comfort above the good of the organization, he will promote this newly nerfed woman to higher and higher leadership positions as long as she makes him and the men above him feel better about themselves. 

Now we have a woman in management who can't be direct, doesn't ask anyone for anything, has no demands, no boundaries, and struggles to meet her KPIs, usually working late into the night and on weekends to force solutions on a team who doesn't trust her, and resents her for her lack of transparency or availability.
